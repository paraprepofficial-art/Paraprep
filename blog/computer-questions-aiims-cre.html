Computer Questions in AIIMS CRE — PYQ Analysis & What to Study

📅 March 2026 ⏱ 8 min read ✍️ ParaPrep Team

Computer questions in AIIMS CRE Section 5 are highly predictable. The same 8–10 topic areas repeat every year — focus here for full marks.

Topic-wise Distribution (PYQ Analysis)

Based on analysis of multiple AIIMS CRE question papers, the same topics repeat every year. Here's the approximate distribution:

MS Office — 3–4 Questions Every Year

This single topic can secure 12–16 marks. Focus on:

  • MS Word shortcuts: Ctrl+B (Bold), Ctrl+I (Italic), Ctrl+Z (Undo), Ctrl+Y (Redo), Ctrl+F (Find), F7 (Spell check)
  • MS Excel: Cell addressing (A1 vs $A$1), SUM/AVERAGE/COUNT/IF formulas, chart types
  • MS PowerPoint: Slide transitions, animation pane, Slide Sorter view, Slide Show (F5)
  • Common to all: Ctrl+S (Save), Ctrl+P (Print), F12 (Save As), Alt+F4 (Close app)

Networking & Internet

  • IP address classes: A (0–127), B (128–191), C (192–223), D (multicast), E (research)
  • Protocols: HTTP (80), HTTPS (443), FTP (21), SMTP (25), POP3 (110), DNS (53)
  • Topologies: Bus, Star, Ring, Mesh, Tree, Hybrid
  • Transmission media: Twisted pair, Coaxial, Fibre optic, Wireless

Computer Fundamentals

  • Generations: 1st (Vacuum tubes) → 5th (AI/parallel processing)
  • Memory: RAM (volatile, primary) vs ROM (non-volatile) vs Cache vs Secondary
  • Storage units: 1 KB = 1024 B, 1 MB = 1024 KB, 1 GB = 1024 MB, 1 TB = 1024 GB
  • CPU components: ALU (arithmetic), CU (control), Registers (fast temporary storage)

Number Systems

  • Binary (base 2), Octal (base 8), Decimal (base 10), Hexadecimal (base 16)
  • Practise conversions: Binary ↔ Decimal ↔ Hex for numbers up to 255
  • Logic gates: AND, OR, NOT, NAND, NOR, XOR — know truth tables
  • 1's complement and 2's complement for negative numbers

✅ 2-week study plan: Week 1 — MS Office (3 days) + Networking (2 days) + Fundamentals (2 days). Week 2 — Number systems + OS + Cybersecurity + 200 MCQ practice on ParaPrep.
