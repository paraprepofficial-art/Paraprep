How to Crack AIIMS CRE 2026 — Complete Paramedical Preparation Guide

📅 March 2026 ⏱️ 8 min read ✍️ ParaPrep Team

The AIIMS Combined Recruitment Exam (CRE) is one of the most competitive paramedical exams in India. With 100 questions, 5 sections, a +4/−1 marking scheme, and section-time locks, it demands a specific strategy — not just hard work. This guide gives you everything you need.

1. Exam Pattern — Know It Perfectly

FeatureDetails
Total Questions100
Total Marks400
Sections5 sections × 20 questions
Time per Section18 minutes (section locks after time up)
Total Time90 minutes
Correct Answer+4 marks
Wrong Answer−1 mark
Unattempted0 marks

⚠️ Section Lock Warning: Once 18 minutes are over for a section, you cannot go back. You must answer all 20 questions in that section before time runs out — even if unsure.

2. What Are the 5 Sections?

For most paramedical posts, the structure is:

  • Sections 1–4: Technical subject (e.g. Optometry, OT Tech, MLT) — 80 questions
  • Section 5: General subjects — Computer, GK, English, Reasoning — 20 questions

The exact technical subject depends on the post you applied for. If you applied for OT Technician, sections 1–4 will be OT-related. For Optometry, they will cover Optometry topics.

3. Section-wise Strategy

Technical Sections (1–4) — 80 Marks

This is where the exam is won or lost. With 80 questions in your core subject, each correct answer gives you 4 marks. Preparing this section well can get you 240–280 marks alone.

  • Practise topic-by-topic — use ParaPrep's Topics MCQ mode to drill each sub-topic separately
  • Focus on PYQ-style questions — AIIMS CRE asks direct, fill-in-the-blank style questions, not tricky ones
  • Target 85%+ accuracy in technical sections for a competitive score
  • Never leave a question blank in a section you know well — 0 on unattempted, −1 on wrong is the same risk, but correct gives +4

General Section (Section 5) — 20 Marks

Computer questions are the most important part of Section 5 — typically 10–12 out of 20 questions come from Computer.

  • Computer: MS Office (Word, Excel, PowerPoint), Networks, OS, Memory, Number Systems — basic and direct questions
  • GK: India-focused — government schemes, history, geography, science
  • English: Antonyms, synonyms, fill-in-the-blank — straightforward vocabulary
  • Reasoning: Series, direction, coding-decoding — speed is key

4. Marking Scheme — When to Attempt

💡 Simple rule: If you are more than 25% sure about an answer, attempt it. The math works in your favour — 1 correct answer (+4) compensates for 4 wrong answers (−1 × 4 = −4).

Never blindly guess — but don't be overly cautious either. If you can eliminate 2 options, your odds are 50-50 and attempting becomes profitable.

5. Preparation Plan

60-Day Plan

  • Days 1–30: Cover all technical topics systematically. Use ParaPrep Topics MCQ to test each topic after studying it.
  • Days 31–45: Focus on weak topics identified from your performance analysis. Take 1 mock test per week.
  • Days 46–55: Full mock tests daily. Review every wrong answer. Analyse patterns.
  • Days 56–60: Revision only — notes, high-yield facts, and light practice.

Daily Practice Routine

  • Morning: 30 minutes — Topics MCQ on your weak subject
  • Evening: Complete the Daily Challenge (10 questions)
  • Weekend: Full 100-question mock test + detailed review

6. Best Resources

  • ParaPrep — 1430+ MCQs, full mock tests with real AIIMS CRE pattern, performance analysis
  • PYQ Papers — Previous year AIIMS CRE papers (CGOT23, CG-OT22)
  • Standard textbooks for your technical subject
  • ParaPrep Telegram — Exam alerts and daily tips

7. Final Tips

  • ✅ Always complete all 20 questions in each section — even if guessing on the last 2
  • ✅ In the exam, spend the first 14 minutes answering questions you know, then use the last 4 minutes for guesses
  • ✅ Read the question carefully — AIIMS CRE often uses fill-in-the-blank format
  • ✅ Stay consistent — daily 1-hour practice beats cramming before the exam
  • ❌ Don't ignore Computer or GK — Section 5 can be the difference between selection and rejection
